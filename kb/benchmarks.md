# Civ 7 progress benchmarks (post Test of Time / Update 1.4.0)

**Purpose.** Give the advisors a yardstick for "where am I, and do I need to push
harder?" at points across each Age, combining (a) static pacing benchmarks and
(b) a *relative* comparison to rival players that should be weighted more and
more heavily as the game goes on.

**Recency is the whole point.** The **Test of Time** update (**Update 1.4.0,
released 19 May 2026**) is the biggest patch the game has had — it reworked
Victories, replaced Legacy Paths with **Triumphs**, made every civ playable in
every Age ("Time-Tested Civs"), and **deliberately cut "yield bloat"** (players
were hitting very high yields with little investment). So *any* guide or
spreadsheet written before mid-May 2026 overstates yields and is not safe to use
as a benchmark. Everything below is anchored on **post-1.4.0** data (Jun 2026).

> Confidence note: 1.4.0 is only ~1 month old as of this writing, so the
> community has **not** published turn-by-turn yield-rate tables yet. The hard
> numbers below are *endpoint snapshots* from post-update competitive games
> (CivFanatics "7OTM" Game-of-the-Month completion threads, June 2026). Per-turn
> yield ranges are **derived estimates** and flagged as such — prefer the
> completion-pace and relative-to-rival checks, which rest on real data.

---

## 1. The single most important framing: victories are *relative*

Under Test of Time the three "Dominance" victories — **Military (Dominion)**,
**Cultural (Tourism)**, **Economic (GDP)** — are won not by hitting a fixed
number but by reaching a **multiple of the second-place player's score** and
holding that lead for **5 turns**. The required multiple shrinks as the game
ages:

| Phase                | Required lead over 2nd place |
|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Exploration, mid     | **6×** |
| Exploration, end     | **4×** |
| Modern, early        | **3×** |
| Modern, mid          | **2×** |
| Modern, late         | **1.5×** |
| Modern, final        | **1.25×** |

**Scientific victory** is absolute instead: accumulate **100 Innovation**, then
**launch a rocket** for a 5-turn countdown. Innovation comes from completing
Projects across all three Ages, slotting Codices, pursuing Tech Masteries, and a
repeatable final Project.

Point sources for the dominance victories:
- **Military / Dominion** — controlling & conquering Settlements, settling in Distant Lands.
- **Cultural / Tourism** — Wonders, Unique Buildings, Unique Infrastructure, Celebrations (and displayed Relics/Artifacts/Great Works).
- **Economic / GDP** — economy growth over time, slotted Resources, Gold Buildings, Treasure Convoys, Factory Resources.

**Implication for the advisors (matches the project goal):** because the win
condition *is* "be N× the second-place empire," once you can see rival scores
you should weight the rival comparison far more heavily than any static
benchmark. You typically do **not** meet the other continent's players until the
**Exploration Age** (Distant Lands), so:

- **Antiquity** → you usually can't see the strongest rivals; lean on the
  **static pacing benchmarks** in §3 as the yardstick.
- **Exploration onward** → switch to **"am I ≥ the required multiple of the
  current second-place player?"** against the schedule above, and treat static
  numbers as only a sanity floor.

## 2. Age pacing (Standard Age length)

Ages don't end on a fixed turn — they end when the Age clock fills, which is
driven by Triumph/Age-progress completion. On Standard length an uncontested Age
can run to roughly **150–160 turns**, but competent players finish sooner by
completing Triumphs. So **the turn you complete an Age is itself the pace
metric.** Typical Modern Age is shorter (~120 turns nominal), and an aggressive
game can end the *whole* match far earlier.

Post-1.4.0 completion turns observed in competitive games:

| Age          | Aggressive / rush | Builder "on pace" | Slow / behind |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Antiquity    | ~T100–122         | ~T120–140         | >T140 |
| Exploration (turns *in* age) | ~T14–50 (military) | ~+55–75 | >+90 |
| Full game to a win | total ~T136 (domination) | total ~T170–210 (culture) | total >T240 |

(Examples: post-update Antiquity completions clustered T100–140; full cultural
victories landed at total T170–243; a domination game ended the whole match at
total T136 with only 14 turns spent in the Modern Age.)

## 3. Endpoint benchmarks by Age (post-1.4.0 competitive snapshots)

These are "what a strong game looks like at the **end** of the Age." Use them in
Antiquity especially, where rival scores aren't yet visible.

### End of Antiquity
- **Settlements:** ~**4 cities** + several towns. Strong builders run **8–12
  total settlements** (often pushing over the soft cap and eating the happiness
  penalty on purpose). Base settlement cap in Antiquity is **3** before
  civics/techs/leader bonuses.
- **Wonders:** **3–6 built** is normal; **5–6** marks a strong wonder/culture
  game (plus any captured).
- **Tourism (cultural score proxy):** leaders ~**80–90**, the field ~**40–50**.
  A winning culture pace is roughly **2× the rest of the field** by Age end.
- **Future Civics** (overflow after finishing the civic tree, = you had time to
  spare): **0–2**. **2** is strong; **0** means you were behind on culture/science.

### End of Exploration
- **Settlements:** ~**6–7 cities** + **12–22 towns** (~**19 total** is common
  for a wide empire). Base cap is **8** before bonuses (carries over higher if
  you ended Antiquity above it).
- **Wonders:** **10–12 controlled** for a culture push.
- **Tourism:** a winning culture game reaches ~**380–440**; a strong non-culture
  game ~**250–300**; AI rivals typically ~**35–70**. (This is where the 4×–6×
  victory multiples in §1 become the live target.)
- **Future Civics:** **1–3**.
- **Relics / City-State suzerainty:** culture winners reported ~**30 relics**
  and **~9 city-states** as suzerain.

### Modern Age
- Base settlement cap **16**. Innovation target for Science victory is **100**.
- Post-1.4.0 endgame Modern data is still thin (games are long and the patch is
  new); use the §1 victory-multiple schedule (3× → 2× → 1.5× → 1.25×) as the
  pacing target rather than fixed yields.

## 4. Derived per-turn yield ranges (LOW CONFIDENCE — estimates)

No post-1.4.0 source publishes per-turn yield rates by turn, and ToT cut yield
bloat, so treat these as rough "order of magnitude" sanity checks only. Prefer
§2 (pace) and §1 (relative) checks.

| Age / point | Science | Culture | Gold (net) | Production (per city) |
|-------------|---------|---------|------------|-----------------------|
| Antiquity early (~T20) | low single digits | low single digits | break-even ± | ~5–10 |
| Antiquity end (~T120) | tens | tens | small surplus | ~15–25 |
| Exploration end | higher tens | higher tens | surplus funds buys/upgrades | ~30+ |

If your yields are *flat* across many turns, or a rival's known score is pulling
away faster than yours, that's the real signal — not the absolute number.

---

## 5. How the advisors should use this

1. **Antiquity:** compare against §3 *End of Antiquity* and §2 pacing. Are you
   tracking toward ~4 cities, your chosen Triumphs, and ~80+ tourism (if going
   culture) by the Age clock? Flag categories that are behind pace.
2. **Exploration & Modern:** once rival scores are visible, compute *your score ÷
   second-place score* and compare to the required multiple for the current
   phase (§1). **Weight this over the static numbers.** "Behind" = below the
   multiple you'll need by Age end; "leading" = at/above it with margin to hold 5
   turns.
3. **Always** read the in-game live scores (`player.Victories`,
   `Game.VictoryManager`) for the relative check, and the Age clock
   (`Game.AgeProgressManager`) for pace.
